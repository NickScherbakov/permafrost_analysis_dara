Business uncertainty analysis using LC-curves  
Fuad T. Aleskerov1, Inna S. Lola1*, Dmitriy G. Asoskov1, Daria A. Zabelina1, Rita 
D. Nazarova1 
1National Research University Higher School of Economics (Moscow, Russia) 
*Corresponding author, email: ilola@hse.ru 
The LC-curve method, a new approach to time series analysis, was applied to 
the composite Business Uncertainty Index ( BUI), based on the results of regular 
Rosstat business surveys, which made it possible to analyze uncertainty trajectories 
across Russia's enlarged industries and sub -sectors using two index specifications: 
ex-ante (forecast) and ex-post (actual). The results of the empirical analysis for the 
period 2020-2024 have shown the high sensitivity of LC -curves to shocks of the 
business environment, their relevance for assessing the stages of business activity in 
various industries. Thus, both crisis periods included in the time interval of the study, 
namely 2020 and 2022, were successfully identified in the dynamics of the plotted 
curves, and it was shown that the pandemic had a more serious negative impact. At 
the same time, according to their forecast dynamics, in 2025 the industry will enter 
a phase of slowing growth. Special attention is paid to the analysis of medium-high 
and high-tech industries, among which the industries that demonstrate the greatest 
stability and potentially play a key role in the structural transformation of the 
economy are highlighted.  
Keywords: LC-curves, business uncertainty, time series.
